Transform Your Garden

Create a haven for wildlife with our expert tips and guides.

Grow a Wildflower Meadow

Wildflower meadows provide a habitat for butterflies, bees, and other pollinators. They are easy to maintain and add vibrant beauty to your garden.

  • Choose a sunny spot in your garden.
  • Prepare the soil by removing existing grass and weeds.
  • Sow a mix of native wildflower seeds suitable for the UK.

Create a Pond for Wildlife

A pond attracts frogs, newts, dragonflies, and provides drinking water for birds and mammals. It also supports aquatic plants and insects.

  • Choose a spot with partial sunlight.
  • Dig a hole with varying depths for different species.
  • Line it with pond liner and fill with rainwater.

Discover Native Species

Red Fox

The red fox is one of the UK's most iconic species. It is adaptable and can thrive in both rural and urban environments.

European Hedgehog

Hedgehogs are vital for gardens as they eat slugs and insects. Provide a safe space with leaves and logs for shelter.

Barn Owl

Known for its distinctive heart-shaped face, the barn owl is often seen hunting in open fields at dusk.

Common Frog

Frogs are a gardener's friend, eating pests like slugs. A small pond can provide them with a perfect habitat.

Bumblebee

Essential pollinators, bumblebees thrive in gardens with a variety of flowers that bloom throughout the seasons.

Pipistrelle Bat

These small bats feed on insects at night and can be encouraged with bat boxes and night-scented flowers.
